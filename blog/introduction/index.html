JULY 22, 2023

Where do I begin?

It's been just shy of a month since I embarked on my journey at TTI. As the company's first DevOps engineer, I've been tasked with a colossal project – transitioning everything from Rackspace to AWS. This move isn't just a simple hop from one platform to another, but a shift in mindset, moving from the traditional VMs to more efficient containers.

It's a big job, no doubt. But, it's the kind of challenge that gets you excited to wake up in the morning. It's like when you're waiting for the delivery of a much-anticipated tech gadget. The anticipation, the possibilities - it's all part of the game.

To keep the team in the loop with the project, I've been posting regular updates on our Slack workspace. Think of it as my tech vlog of sorts, where I share what's going on behind the scenes. It's a good way to keep everyone informed, and hopefully, answer a few questions before they're even asked. Plus, it allows the team to jump in if they see something they'd like to discuss further. It's a bit like live-streaming my thought process - unfiltered, raw, and real.

What I didn't expect, though, is how much writing all this down actually helped me. It's turned out to be a sort of "rubber duck debugging" exercise. If you're not familiar with the concept, it's where you explain your code line by line to a rubber duck (or any inanimate object, really) in an effort to spot any errors or ways to improve it. Except, in my case, I'm not talking to a rubber duck, but to a whole team of fellow tech enthusiasts.

After some time, I realized that I was sitting on a ton of material that could be valuable to others outside of our Slack workspace. All these notes, all this "rubber duck debugging" could be lost in the infinite scroll of Slack messages. That's when it hit me. Why not share my experiences, my day-to-day encounters, and discoveries, with a broader audience?

So, here we are. If you've ever wondered what it's like to be a DevOps engineer, or are just interested in tech and how it shapes our world, stick around. I'll be pulling back the curtain on my work, sharing the highs and lows, the challenges, and the triumphs, in a way that I hope will be interesting and insightful. Let's jump in, shall we?
